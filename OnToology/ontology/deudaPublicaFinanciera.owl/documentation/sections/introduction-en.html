Introduction back to ToC

The Public Debt of a City Council is made up of the Public Financial Debt and the Public Commercial Debt. This vocabulary represents the data of the Financial Public Debt. The vocabulary of commercial public debt can be consulted at http://vocab.ciudadesabiertas.es/def/economia/deuda-publica-comercial/index-es.html. The public debt of a city council is one of the main indicators of public accounts that helps to understand the its economic situation. In macroeconomic terms, it is also a conditioning factor for spending, saving, public investment, and economic policy itself. Public debt is decisive for, for example, the preparation of budgets. Likewise, the level of public sector indebtedness is determined by the expenditure ceiling established according to the Organic Law 2/2012, of April 27, on Budgetary Stability and Financial Sustainability (LOEPSF). Specifically, for local entities there is the Royal Decree-Law 17/2014, of December 26, on financial sustainability measures of the autonomous communities and local entities and others of an economic nature. These regulations define several objectives related to budgetary stability, the most relevant of which is to guarantee the budgetary stability and financial sustainability of all Public Administrations, which means that all levels of Public Administrations are obliged to comply with the LOEPSF. The annual debt of an entity is related to financing instruments such as loans and issuances. For both financing instruments, there is a repayment schedule with dates and amounts. A loan can be granted by one or more lenders. The financial burden of a financing instrument corresponds to certain financial expenses and liabilities and on a certain date it has a living capital.
